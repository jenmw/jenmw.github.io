eBay Mobile App Redesign

I’m very passionate about buying used clothes so I use the eBay app every day. I quite often come across frustrating experiences as a user myself, so I decided I could create a project around redesigning the app myself. This redesign task aims to gather data to identify pain points and create user stories to inform a new design that will solve those pain points and modernise the app with current UI and UX trends.

Ideally I would be able to use large sets of user data collected by eBay to inform the design work, but since this is a personal project and not affiliated with eBay, I decided to the eBay Community Message Boards. From the Apple and Android boards I managed to get responses from 11 app users.

When asked what task they use the app for most, 73% said they use the app for buying compared to 27% for selling. So for the purpose of this project I’ve focused more on features related to buying than selling.

I also wanted to gauge how important certain things were to users on screen so that I could establish some kind of hierarchy of information. So I asked users to say, from a limited list of features, whether they used them “most or every time”, “occasionally”, “rarely”, or “never”.

The results show that, unsurprisingly, features related to selling rank low since most of the users in my sample said they were predominantly buyers. The two highest ranking features are saved searches and watched items, so this project will focus on those two features in particular and how to reflect their importance to users. Interestingly, some features aimed at buying such as today’s deals ranked very low, suggesting that this feature isn’t valued by users, though admittedly my sample is very small and would need greater analysis to confirm.

From the survey results I generated two user stories based on the two most commonly used features.

On a more personal note, I wanted the app feel more modern, so I’ve added a few UI and branding notes on palettes and icons.

Kept the main colours the same, added some complimentary swatches. The secondary palette incorporates some deeper, richer, earthier tones. Many signs point towards earth tones being a trend for 2020. Pantone 2020 Colour of the Year is a very deep blue! The lighter swatches in the Highlights palette for accents pair very nicely with the Secondary swatches.

Heavy blocks of colour offset nicely with icons that are lightweight, minimal and slightly rounded.
